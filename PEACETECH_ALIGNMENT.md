# PeaceTech Alignment

## Primary Track

`Voice & accountability`

## One-line Business Case

Civic Access Navigator lowers the cost and risk of civic participation by turning rights information into safe, incident-specific accountability actions.

## Why This Track Fits

The product is most effective as a `Voice & accountability` tool because it focuses on the gap between citizens and the institutions meant to serve them.

It promotes this theme by:

- translating rights and civic information into plain language
- helping citizens ask better questions before approaching institutions
- surfacing trusted public-interest sources instead of rumor or guesswork
- offering a quick path to escalation through chat and SOS actions
- creating a reusable accountability workflow that can expand from Kenya into other African contexts

## Benchmark Against OSF Focus Areas

### Democratic Practice

This is the strongest OSF fit.

CAN promotes democratic practice by:

- helping citizens prepare for engagement with institutions in plain language
- reducing the friction of asking questions, documenting incidents, and escalating concerns
- building a safer path to accountability in environments where power is often opaque

### Rights and Dignity

This is also a strong fit.

CAN supports rights and dignity by:

- converting abstract rights into incident-specific next steps
- reducing the risk of seeking help in fragile settings
- protecting user dignity through low-friction, safety-aware interaction patterns

### Equity in Governance

This is a meaningful secondary fit.

CAN supports equity in governance by:

- lowering information asymmetry for users closest to exclusion and conflict
- making governance-facing information more accessible to displaced and low-bandwidth users
- creating a reusable workflow that can localize across countries without rebuilding the product logic

### Justice

This is a supporting fit through the accountability layer.

CAN supports justice by:

- creating a traceable path from incident to reportable action
- enabling `send-when-connected` accountability steps in unstable environments
- preparing the ground for future advocacy, legal follow-up, or verified observer escalation

## Overall OSF Positioning

The strongest honest positioning is:

> Civic Access Navigator is most directly aligned with OSF's **Democratic Practice** and **Rights and Dignity** focus areas, while also contributing to **Equity in Governance** and **Justice** through safer, traceable accountability workflows.

## Product Boundaries

This prototype does not claim to replace emergency responders, lawyers, or community mediators.

It is designed to:

- orient users quickly
- guide them toward trusted information
- help them decide what accountable next step to take

## Documentation-only Concepts

The following concepts remain important to the project, but no longer appear as primary UI chips on the home screen:

- Language-aware
- Doctrines-aware
- Culturally local

They remain part of the design and expansion logic, but are now expressed in documentation and system behavior rather than as hero-level labels.
