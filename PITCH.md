# Pitch

## Title

Civic Access Navigator

## Short Pitch

Civic Access Navigator is an OSF-aligned PeaceTech capstone that helps people in fragile environments move from understanding a right to invoking that right through safe, incident-specific workflows. It is designed first and foremost as a `Voice & accountability` tool for supporting conflict-affected communities while showing how prompt-aware development can keep the system grounded and reviewable.

## Why It Matters

Important peacebuilding and civic information is often difficult to locate, fragmented across sources, or written in language that is hard to act on under pressure. This project lowers that barrier and supports safer community action and accountability.

## Business Case

Business case in one line: Civic Access Navigator lowers the cost and risk of civic participation by turning rights information into safe, incident-specific accountability actions.

The product wins if it becomes a reusable accountability layer that can localize by country and language while keeping one trusted source backbone. That gives OSF and partners a tool they can reuse across contexts instead of rebuilding from zero every time.

## Theme Fit

The clearest hackathon fit is `Voice & accountability`.

The tool promotes that theme by:

- helping citizens understand rights and civic processes quickly
- guiding people toward trusted sources before they approach institutions
- making escalation and help-seeking easier through chat and SOS actions
- reducing the distance between communities and public-interest systems

## What We Are Building

- a lightweight incident-workflow interface for crisis and accountability scenarios
- a curated rights and civic database that sits behind the workflow layer
- a path toward grounded question-answering over trusted sources
- dynamic CTA actions that move users from knowing a right to invoking a right
- a send-when-connected accountability queue for low-bandwidth environments
- a prompt-aware build loop with checks for clarity, safety, and source alignment
- a Kenya-first prototype that can expand across Africa
- a visual, mobile-friendly interface with regional and language adaptability

## Why Codex

Codex helps accelerate scaffolding, iteration, backend logic, frontend changes, and documentation for a capstone that needs to move from concept to working prototype quickly. It also helps us keep prompts, outputs, and verification steps aligned as the project moves through the SDLC.
