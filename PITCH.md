# Pitch

## Title

Civic Access Navigator

## Short Pitch

Civic Access Navigator is an OSF-aligned PeaceTech capstone that helps people find and understand trusted peace, civic, and accountability information in plain language. It is designed as a practical AI-assisted tool for supporting conflict-affected communities while showing how prompt-aware development can keep the system grounded and reviewable.

## Why It Matters

Important peacebuilding and civic information is often difficult to locate, fragmented across sources, or written in language that is hard to interpret quickly. This project lowers that barrier and supports more informed community action and accountability.

## Business Case

The product wins if it becomes a reusable peace-navigation layer that can localize by country, language, and doctrine while keeping one trusted source backbone. That gives OSF and partners a tool they can reuse across contexts instead of rebuilding from zero every time.

## What We Are Building

- a lightweight peace and civic information interface
- a curated resource library organized by track and region
- a path toward grounded question-answering over trusted sources
- a prompt-aware build loop with checks for clarity, safety, and source alignment
- a Kenya-first prototype that can expand across Africa
- a visual, mobile-friendly interface with regional and language adaptability

## Why Codex

Codex helps accelerate scaffolding, iteration, backend logic, frontend changes, and documentation for a capstone that needs to move from concept to working prototype quickly. It also helps us keep prompts, outputs, and verification steps aligned as the project moves through the SDLC.
