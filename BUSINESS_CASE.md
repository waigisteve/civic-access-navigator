# Business Case

## What This Product Solves

Peace and civic information is often fragmented, hard to search, and not tailored to the local language, conflict context, or community worldview. Civic Access Navigator solves that by turning curated peace resources into a region-aware, AI-assisted entry point for communities, field teams, and advocates.

## Why It Can Win

The strongest version of this product is not just a chatbot. It is a practical peace navigation layer that helps people:

- understand what peace means in their context
- compare local and regional perspectives without losing nuance
- find trusted resources faster
- move from confusion to action
- reuse one platform across many countries with different languages and doctrines

## Business Value

- lowers research time for users who need trusted peace information quickly
- improves access for communities that are often excluded by language or technical barriers
- creates a reusable framework for multiple countries instead of a one-country tool
- supports OSF field partners with a consistent interface across regions
- can expand from Kenya into East Africa, the Sahel, the DRC, Sudan, Mozambique, and beyond

## Adaptability Model

The platform is designed around three layers:

1. Region layer: country, subregion, and continent views
2. Language layer: local language packs and translation-aware prompts
3. Meaning layer: different community understandings of peace, justice, recovery, and accountability

That means one interface can adapt to many places without erasing local context.

## Success Criteria

- users can ask a question and get a grounded answer with citations
- the system can switch region and language without rewriting the app
- the interface remains visually engaging and easy to scan on phone and desktop
- the content model can support multiple countries and peace traditions

## Long-Term Outcome

If this works well, it becomes a repeatable PeaceTech product pattern for OSF and partner networks: fast to localize, safe to use, and strong enough to support trust across borders.
